Advanced Typography & Micro-AestheticsYou must see text not as words, but as an architectural system of positive and negative space.Kerning & Tracking: Spotting awkward character collisions or gaps. You must know when to apply global letter-spacing adjustments (tracking) via CSS letter-spacing and when to manually slice text into components to inject surgical, absolute-positioned kerning adjustments for god-tier hero headlines.Variable Fonts & Fluid Type: Evaluating if the site uses fluid typography scales based on modern CSS functions like clamp(minimum, preferred, maximum). You should instantly spot if a font's weight, width, or optical size fails to smoothly adapt to changing screen dimensions.Hierarchical Modular Scales: Identifying the mathematical ratio (e.g., the Golden Ratio 1.618 or Perfect Fourth 1.333) governing the type scale. You should immediately call out when an h1, h2, and body text feel disconnected or arbitrarily sized.Reading Comfort Dynamics: Judging line height (line-height) relative to line length (measure). The ideal measure is 45 to 75 characters per line; anything wider strains the eye, and anything shorter breaks reading flow.2. Spatial Mathematics & Layout SystemsGod-tier reviewers treat layout as a strict, predictable system of invisible grids and boundaries.The 8pt / 4pt Grid System: Spotting padding, margins, and component heights that deviate from multiples of 4px or 8px. Consistent spatial intervals create a subconscious sense of order and premium quality.Flexbox & Grid Alignment Integrity: Catching microscopic alignment errors. You must notice if items aren't perfectly centered along baseline axes, or if grid gaps (gap) scale clumsily on ultrawide monitors versus mobile screens.Visual Weight & Hierarchy: Analyzing the page with a "squint test" to evaluate contrast. The most critical user actions must visually anchor the page, while secondary elements fade gracefully into the background.3. Motion, Choreography & Interaction Micro-DosagesMovement on a premium website should mimic physical realities—never robotic, never jarring.Eased Transition Kinetics: Detecting linear or default CSS animations. A master demands custom cubic-bezier curves (like cubic-bezier(0.25, 1, 0.5, 1)) for ultra-smooth easing that mimics inertia and friction.Interface Choreography: Evaluating the sequence of animations. When a menu opens, its internal elements should cascade in a tight, intentional stagger (e.g., 20ms delays between list items) rather than appearing all at once.State Feedback Loop: Critiquing interactive elements (hover, focus, active, disabled states). A button must respond instantly to a hover with subtle depth, color elevation, or scale changes, signaling its exact interactive capability.4. Psychological Friction & UX StrategyDesign is entirely meaningless if it confuses the human brain or slows down user intent.Cognitive Load & Miller’s Law: Identifying areas cluttering the user’s working memory. You must suggest grouping chaotic information into clean, bite-sized modules of no more than 5–7 chunks.Fitts’s Law Calibration: Judging the size and placement of interactive target areas. Mobile buttons must be easily reachable by a thumb and feature a tap target area of at least 48x48px to prevent frustrating mis-taps.Jakob’s Law Alignment: Balancing creative expression with familiar patterns. If a site completely reinvents standard navigation paradigms (like hiding a shopping cart icon in an unexpected corner), you must flag it as an unnecessary hurdle to conversion.5. Technical Performance & Engineering EmpathyA beautiful design that loads slowly or breaks on a mid-range phone is an objective failure.Layout Shifts (CLS): Spotting elements that twitch or jump as images and fonts finish loading. You must propose hard-coding aspect ratios or using skeleton loaders to achieve a Cumulative Layout Shift score of zero.Asset Optimization Physics: Auditing media delivery. You must call out uncompressed PNGs or heavy video formats, demanding modern web standards like WebP/AVIF images and hardware-accelerated SVG animations.Accessibility (a11y) Rigor: Scanning color contrast ratios against WCAG AAA standards.
